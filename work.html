Our work

Building a new bridge between government and private sector innovation.

We solve problems by connecting government and companies to collaborate more efficiently and effectively to build next-generation solutions that can't be found anywhere else. One way we do this is through our Joint Venture Partners program.

NTIS Joint Venture Partners Capabilities

New Technologies

New technical capability or private sets to help statistical agencies derive economic, demographic and social insights from Federal and private sector data. Our partners may enable federal statistical data-centric agencies to create new capabilities to derive insights from national economic, social, and demographic data sets in both the federal government and the private sector. The focus here is on new capabilities, and the derived insights will be downstream innovation.

Better Public Services

Assistance through the integration of public and private data, the “Internet of Things,” cloud computing and predictive analytics, to derive insights for land use planning, and delivering improved public services. Our partners may create new capabilities to support Smart City initiatives. Cities and communities, working with land use planners, will apply new capabilities in data science and predictive analytics to support improvements in transportation, environment, health and safety, and environment.

Become a Joint Venture Partner

Join us in a unique industry-government venture, helping transform data into insights and actions. Our partners can benefit from new business opportunities with federal customers. Our partners represent the best and the brightest industry offers for federal data products and services across a variety of domains.

We select partners based on merit and are open to for-profit organization, non-profit organization, and research-performing service organizations.

Our partners have the opportunity to work on innovative and challenging data projects which:

  • Support federal data priorities such as big data, open data, open access, Smart Cities, Internet-of-Things, and cyber-physical systems
  • Use federal data, either alone or in combination with non-federal data, to fuel economic growth, improve public services, or increase the efficiency of government operations
  • Involve emerging technology, technical complexity, agile development and operations, or unique requirements

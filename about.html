About Us

Every year, the government sponsors billions of dollars of scientific research.

The data that comes from this research can foster innovation, stimulate the economy, and lead to new jobs. But businesses, government agencies, and members of the public need to access it first.

The National Technical Information Service houses the world's most comprehensive collection of U.S.-sponsored research. With more than 3 million publications, our data covers topics that range from aerodynamics to urban development. Our archive spans the Manhattan project to present.

No other group can replicate our history of managing data, and we use that institutional knowledge to help federal agencies make their data public.

Our team:

  • Delivers agile data services to government agencies
  • Builds partnerships with the private sector
  • Takes privacy and security seriously

Deliver Agile Data Services to Government Agencies

Agencies turn to us when they have complex data challenges. Our team works fast and in the open, helping them create elegant, scalable solutions. By innovating together, we illuminate vital information about the U.S. economy, population, and environment.

Build Partnerships with the Private Sector

Businesses, communities, and the public depend on government information to advance the economy, to solve problems, and to create jobs. Our team makes government data easier to access for these groups.

Take Privacy and Security Seriously

Our team handles all information with the highest standards of privacy and security. We only support trusted data networks. We follow rigorous privacy and security guidelines.

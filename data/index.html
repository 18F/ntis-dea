Big Data in the Federal Government

The federal government is a leading source of the nation’s Big Data. Our agencies collect, compile, assess and deliver some of the world’s most reliable data on every conceivable aspect of our nation.

Within the federal government, The U.S. Commerce Department is known as “America’s Data Agency” because it delivers 40 percent of all federal data. People use Commerce data for insight into their weather and climate, their communities, and how the economy is faring, make investment and hiring decisions and warn of coming danger, position first-responders, construct high-tech classrooms, and more. The federal government uses Commerce data to allocate funds and make critical decisions on fiscal and monetary policy.

Within Commerce, NTIS delivers the most advanced scientific and technological data in the world, known for over 100 years for its accuracy and precision. Given how data fuels today’s world, NTIS joins the rest of government in striving to make our data easier to use—easier to combine and access different data types, standards, methodologies, websites, architecture, platforms, and formats. We’re making progress.

Joint Venture Partnerships

But we know government’s Big Data vision is impossible if we make our data available only through conventional means. So NTIS is embracing public-private sector partnerships as a way to accelerate our data usability goals in ways the federal government can’t do alone efficiently and cost-effectively.

Through our Joint Venture Partnerships program, we help the federal government team up with the private sector to harness data to help our agencies run faster, better, smarter—and serve the public, our customers, even more efficiently and effectively.
